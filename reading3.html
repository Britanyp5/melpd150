Reading and Written Response #3: “Graphic Novel: Motherhood in crisis” by Laurence Ivil, Alicia Prager and Saidu Bah

“Graphic Novel: Motherhood in crisis” by Laurence Ivil, Alicia Prager and Saidu Bah is about Four different women sharing their stories of hardship and survival in Sierra Leone through pregnancy. Reading each story was heartbreaking in a different way because these women were in so much need for something we call basic resources but for them it's literally a rare occurrence to have these things. These stories especially touched my heart as a mother, because I had a bad experience with medical and family support, I can't imagine how it would have been without it. To talk about the artwork it was very beautiful and the way that it expressed the stories through the color and shapes. It gave the reader a solid visual on the situations that were happening which in a way connects the reader and person whom the story is about.

Memunatu’s story ‘Birth in my village’ was scary because she had no help in birthing a child with someone knowledgeable and because of this she struggled. The “lie lie nurse” hadn't helped her properly which just hurt her because she was doing things such as putting her own hand inside of her trying to yank the baby out. Without any medication she was at a loss. After losing the baby she was physically and mentally not okay. Physically she was in so much pain and had no one to talk to about it or help her heal. She was scared to talk to other women in her village from the fear of feeling ashamed of what happened to her. Mentally she was hurt about losing her child, but I feel like she didn't even have the proper time to grieve about her child because she was in so much pain she focused on herself.

Heela The schoolgirl ‘My stolen future’Story about a young girl who was Unfortunately raped. At first I thought it was just child birth that was very dangerous but apparently getting an abortion was also dangerous. That's shocking to me because hereAn abortion is a procedure that is available. Heela Had two outcomesEitherDie to not have the baby or have the baby andLose what's left of her future.She had been shut downFrom many people in her communityAnd this must've been extremely hard for her tooAnd do it but with the resources that were given to her she was able toAt least have someone to trust in.These situations are very heartbreaking because she did not want to have a kid yet she is suffering the consequences. And the man was not captured and punished for his actions which is extremely irritating.

Kadiatu, The grieving mother ‘The devil at my door’ story was Concerning because it shows how their own beliefs canBe a negative impact towards a womanTheir community had chosen to believe in the rumors and a "witch” That she was in some sort of ritual or try to do some wrongdoing to her own children. Because of this many people tried to ignore herIt was shunned out from the community. It would've been a much different story if they had just tried to comfort her or looked for someone that could truly help her and her children.

Tetteh, The prisoner ‘My punishment’. Her story made me angry because it shows how justice is not served towards the right person. Tetteh had been abused by a man she wanted to love but she's the one that ended up in prison, because she accidentally killed them when trying to defend herself. If I had the right to mandate if she were to go to prison or not I would Not let her go to prison because she must have suffered so many traumatic experiences that led to that point and it's not like she wanted to kill him it was just a self-defense mechanism as a human being. To make matters even worse. He would hit her even more when she got pregnant, going as far to even kick her belly. He was not being in any sort of way productive because he couldn't get a job himself and then he was stealing her money that she worked for and then beat her. It makes no sense for that man to treat her so badly. She probably couldn't run away from the situation cause he even made her move with him.

These four stories show the struggles of a woman in an underdeveloped country and because of this they have to go through traumatic experiences that shaped their future in a negative way. A combination of underdeveloped medical practices, too many beliefs inSacred practices,and a community that is not empathetic shuts the doors for so many people.
