Reading and Written Response #4: “Nuclear War: The Rising Risk, and How We Stop It”

This article was very significant in the way it expressed the dangers of nuclear wars through its art and reasoning. The art throughout this article was very detailed because it pointed out many issues with war through the colors and shapes in the image. The dynamic between the reader understanding the text through the art by scrolling is genius. The html command of continue to show art as you scroll down makes the reader want to take it all in. The New York Times interactive article basically talks about how the risk of nuclear war is starting to become more serious again, even though most people do not really think about it anymore. It explains how problems between countries like the United States, Russia, China, and North Korea, mixed with advanced technology and weaker agreements between countries, make the chances of a nuclear conflict more dangerous.

The art in the article is important because it makes the topic feel intense and real, not just informational. The dark background creates a serious mood right away and makes the reader feel like they are entering a dangerous situation. The bright colors, especially red, stand out because they remind people of warnings, emergencies, explosions, and danger.
